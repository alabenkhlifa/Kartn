## 3. CHARGING SPEEDS & TYPES

### Available Charging Types in Tunisia

| Type                      | Power    | Charge Time (0-80%) | Typical Location            | Connector    |
|---------------------------|----------|---------------------|-----------------------------|--------------|
| **Slow (AC Level 1)**     | 3-7 kW   | 8-12 hours          | Home, some public           | Type 2       |
| **Standard (AC Level 2)** | 11-22 kW | 3-6 hours           | Public stations, businesses | Type 2       |
| **Fast (DC Level 3)**     | 40-50 kW | 45-90 min           | TotalEnergies stations      | CCS, CHAdeMO |
| **Rapid (DC)**            | 100+ kW  | 20-40 min           | Very limited                | CCS          |

### Most Common in Tunisia

- **Domestic**: 7 kW / 11 kW / 22 kW (AC)
- **Public**: 22 kW (AC) and 40 kW (DC)
- **Fast chargers (100 kW+)**: Very rare, mainly at select TotalEnergies stations

### Connector Standards

- **Type 2**: Standard for AC charging (European standard)
- **CCS (Combined Charging System)**: For DC fast charging
- **CHAdeMO**: Some Japanese vehicles (less common)

## 4. CHARGING COSTS

### Current Pricing Structure

- **Public charging**: Currently **FREE** at most ANME stations
- **TotalEnergies**: Pay-per-use (pricing varies)
- **Dealerships**: Often free during business hours
- **Home charging**: STEG tariff (~0.15-0.30 TND/kWh depending on tier)

### Estimated Running Costs

| Scenario                    | Cost per 100 km |
|-----------------------------|-----------------|
| Home charging (STEG tariff) | ~3-4 TND        |
| Public AC charging          | ~5-7 TND        |
| DC fast charging            | ~8-12 TND       |
| **Comparison: Gasoline**    | ~15-20 TND      |
| **Comparison: Diesel**      | ~10-14 TND      |

**Note**: EV charging prices are **deregulated** - government does not set fixed rates. Pricing will be determined by
market/operators.

## 5. HOME CHARGING INSTALLATION

### Equipment Costs (2025)

| Charger Type               | Price (TND)   | Use Case              |
|----------------------------|---------------|-----------------------|
| Standard domestic (3.7 kW) | 2,000 - 4,000 | Basic home charging   |
| Accelerated (7-22 kW AC)   | 5,000 - 8,000 | Faster home/business  |
| Fast DC (50 kW)            | 20,000+       | Commercial only       |
| Rapid DC (100 kW)          | ~400,000      | Public infrastructure |

### Installation Requirements

1. **STEG Authorization**: Mandatory for all grid-connected chargers
2. **Professional Installation**: Must use certified electrician
3. **Electrical Upgrade**: May need panel upgrade for 11-22 kW
4. **Installation Cost**: Additional 1,000-3,000 TND for labor/materials

### Popular Home Charger Brands in Tunisia

- **Wallbox** (distributed by Ennakl Automobiles)
- **BYD** chargers (via Hélios Cars)
- **Konner & Sohnen**
- Various Chinese imports

## 9. FCR IMPORT CONSIDERATIONS FOR ELECTRIC VEHICLES

### ⚠️ Should You Import an EV via FCR?

**PROS:**

- Lower running costs (3-4 TND/100km vs 15-20 TND for gasoline)
- Tax incentives make EVs more affordable
- Environmental benefits
- Growing infrastructure

**CONS:**

- Very limited public charging network
- Long charging times at most stations
- No fast chargers outside major cities
- Spare parts availability uncertain
- Battery replacement costs high
- Resale value uncertain in Tunisia

### Recommendation Matrix

| Your Situation                           | Recommendation                       |
|------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| Live in Tunis with home parking          | ✅ EV possible (install home charger) |
| Live in Sousse/Nabeul with home charging | ✅ EV possible                        |
| Long daily commute (>100 km)             | ⚠️ Consider hybrid instead           |
| Live in interior regions                 | ❌ Not recommended for pure EV        |
| No home charging possible                | ❌ Not recommended                    |
| Frequent intercity travel                | ⚠️ Hybrid recommended                |

### Best EV Choices for Tunisia (if importing)

1. **Hyundai Kona Electric** - Good dealer support
2. **BYD Dolphin / ATTO 3** - Local importer support
3. **MG EZS** - Affordable, local presence
4. **Plug-in Hybrids** (safer choice):
    - BYD Song PLUS DM-i
    - Toyota RAV4 Hybrid
    - Kia Niro PHEV
