## üîç PART 1: VISITE TECHNIQUE (Technical Inspection)

### What Is It?

A mandatory inspection to verify that your imported vehicle meets Tunisian safety and environmental standards as defined
by the Code de la Route.

### When Is It Required?

| Situation                           | Timing                                 |
|-------------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| **Imported vehicle (FCR or other)** | **IMMEDIATELY** before immatriculation |
| **New car bought locally**          | After 4 years from first registration  |
| **Used car (periodic)**             | Every 2 years                          |
| **Vehicles >10 years old**          | Every 1 year                           |
| **Taxis, transport vehicles**       | Every 6 months                         |

‚ö†Ô∏è **Critical for FCR**: Your imported car **cannot be registered** without passing visite technique first!

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Issues & Solutions

### Problem 1: Vehicle Fails Visite Technique

**Causes**: Worn brakes, bad lights, excessive emissions, modified exhaust

**Solution**:

1. Get detailed failure report
2. Repair at certified garage
3. Return for counter-visit within 48h (free) or later (paid)

### Problem 2: Documents Don't Match

**Cause**: Chassis number on car ‚â† documents

**Solution**:

1. Verify at customs immediately
2. May need expert verification
3. Can delay registration significantly

### Problem 3: Non-Standard Modifications

**Cause**: Aftermarket parts, tinted windows, lowered suspension

**Solution**:

1. Some modifications prohibited entirely
2. Others need ATTT approval (proc√®s-verbal de r√©ception)
3. May need to restore to original spec

### Problem 4: GPL/GNC Imported Vehicle

**Cause**: Gas installation needs separate certification

**Solution**:

1. Get ATTT attestation for gas system compliance
2. Additional inspection required
3. Must meet Tunisian safety standards
